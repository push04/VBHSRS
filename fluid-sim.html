Artificial Compressibility (Chorin 1967)

Incompressible Navier–Stokes normally needs a pressure Poisson solve every step — expensive and iterative. Chorin's method replaces it with ∂p/∂τ = −β²∇·u. Pressure evolves pseudo-dynamically, pushing the flow toward zero-divergence without any iterative solver. β is an artificial "speed of sound"; when M = U/β ≪ 1, the flow looks nearly incompressible. Trade-off: transient pressure propagates at speed β (not ∞), so short-duration pressure transients are not exactly physical.

Smagorinsky SGS Eddy-Viscosity Model

DNS resolves every turbulent scale — prohibitively expensive. LES (large-eddy simulation) resolves large eddies and models sub-grid ones. The Smagorinsky model adds eddy viscosity ν_t = (Cs·Δ)²·|S̄| — proportional to grid spacing Δ and the local strain-rate magnitude |S̄|. This damps motions the grid cannot resolve. Known limitation: over-dissipates near walls (too much ν_t in boundary layers where the true SGS contribution is small). Improved variants: dynamic Smagorinsky, WALE model, σ-model.

What This Is (and Isn't)

Educational 2D LES toy — not a sign-off CFD code. 2D turbulence has an inverse energy cascade (energy flows to larger scales), opposite to 3D. Real train aero is 3D and needs RANS/LES codes (OpenFOAM, STAR-CCM+, Ansys Fluent) with wall functions, proper 3D meshing, and validation against wind-tunnel data for engineering decisions.
